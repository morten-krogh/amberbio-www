Microarrays

DNA microarrays are micro scale devices used for the detection of nucleic acid samples in complex mixtures. The microarray technique is used to study gene expression, DNA mutations, chromosomal rearrangements, promoter analysis, ChIP-chip, epigenetics and alternative splicing among others in fields ranging from basic science to toxicogenomics and drug discovery.

Due to the size, cost and complexity of microarray experiments, it is crucial to employ proper bioinformatical methods to achieve maximal biological insight. We assist you in all steps of the process including experimental design, data transformation, normalization, clustering, probe annotations, statistics, identification of differentially expressed genes, and machine learning. Furthermore, we can give advice on the use of a large number of software applications as well.
