Statistics

Most experiments have unique characteristics, such as a varying number of technical replicates nested within biological replicates. There are almost always some missing values, and some samples have higher quality than others. Many experiments are not correctly analysed with a t-test or ANOVA and needs a more advanced individual statistical treatment.

Medical diagnostics from microarray or proteomics data has become very important in recent year. The problem is to give a diagnosis or prognosis of patients given their amount of DNA, RNA or protein. This problem can be solved by use of a statistical methodology called machine learning.

We offer to assist researchers with all types of statical analysis of their data including writing computer programs that automate the process.
