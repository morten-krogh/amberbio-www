Sequence analysis

High-throughput DNA sequencing methods, like pyrosequencing, chain ligation methods, or sequencing with reversible terminators, are becoming faster, cheaper and more widespread. Sequencing has many applications including disease association studies and environmental studies of microorganisms through fragments of their genomes. The resulting explosion of new sequences necessitates faster and better computational approaches such as alignments and searches in databases.

We assist you along the process from base calling to annotation of genes. To achieve results of highest possible quality, we offer to screen sequences for vector and environmental contaminants and to remove chimeras and low quality sequence segments. We also perform clustering of similar sequences, like ESTs into UniGene entries, and mapping of ribosomal RNAs to strains or species. The results would typically be stored in a database which can be accessed by the customer through a web-interface. We also develop algorithms for identification of special sequence traits like stable RNAs, transcription factor binding sites or repeats.
