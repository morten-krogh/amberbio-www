Proteomics

Proteomics is the study of a large number of proteins simultaneously as opposed to the study of a few proteins at a time. The most common proteomics techniques are 1D gels, 2D gels, isoelectric focusing, liquid chromatography, mass spectrometry and antibody based methods. In order to extract full information from the experiments, it is necessary to employ a number of bioinformatical methods such as signal normalization, correction for dye effects, treatment of missing values, calibration and scoring of mass spectra, proper experimental design, and statistical analysis of the results. The work flow in a laboratory can also be sped up by having proper software that facilitates the transition from one file format to another.

We offer assistance with all the issues mentioned above and other bioinformatical and statistical problems that occur during the proteomics work flow.
